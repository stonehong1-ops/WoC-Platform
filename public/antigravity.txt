<p class="first-letter:text-7xl first-letter:font-display-lg first-letter:text-primary first-letter:mr-3 first-letter:float-left">
                    In the heart of Buenos Aires, beneath the dim amber glow of a crystal chandelier, the room breathes. There is no speech here, yet the air is thick with dialogue. This is the Milonga—the social dance of tango where legs intertwine like cursive script on a parchment of polished wood. 
                </p>
<p>
                    To an outsider, tango is a spectacle of sharp movements and stern faces. But to the initiates, it is a "silent conversation." It starts with the <i>Cabeceo</i>, the subtle nod of the head from across the room that asks, "May I?" without a single syllable uttered. It is a negotiation of space, weight, and intent that exists only in the present moment.
                </p>

<h3 class="font-headline-md text-headline-md text-on-surface mb-4">The Geometry of Connection</h3>
<p class="font-body-md text-body-md">
                            The dance floor is a grid, yet the dancers move in circles. This geometric paradox is what defines the tango. Each step is an exploration of the partner's balance, a testing of the waters before diving into a complex sequence of <i>ganchos</i> and <i>boleo</i>.
                        </p>
</div>
</div>
<p>
                    The music of the <i>Orquesta Típica</i> dictates the tempo, but the dancers dictate the emotion. When the bandoneón sighs, the couple pauses. This is the <i>parada</i>—the moment of stillness where the silent conversation becomes a shared meditation. In these few seconds, the world outside the Milonga ceases to exist.
                </p>
<!-- Large Quote Section -->
<blockquote class="border-l-4 border-primary pl-8 py-4 my-16 italic font-headline-md text-on-surface bg-surface-container-low rounded-r-xl">
                    "Tango is not about the feet. It is about the space between two heartbeats, synchronized by the breath of a bandoneón."
                    <footer class="mt-4 font-label-md not-italic text-primary">— Maestro Julián Castro</footer>
</blockquote>
<p>
                    As the night deepens, the atmosphere shifts from technical precision to raw sentiment. The last <i>tanda</i> of the night is always the most intimate. Here, the silent conversation reaches its climax. There is no need for flashy maneuvers; a simple walk, executed with total presence, says more than a thousand intricate turns ever could.
                </p>
<p>
                    When the music finally fades, the couple detaches. A simple "Gracias" is the only vocalization permitted. They walk back to their tables, the conversation over, but the echo of the dance remaining in their muscle memory—a secret shared in the dark, soon to be repeated with the next nod of the head.
                </p>
